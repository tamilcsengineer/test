⚖️ Know Your Rights
Women Safety Laws
Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act
Provides protection to women from domestic violence including physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse. Allows women to obtain protection orders, residence orders, and monetary relief.
Act 2005
Sexual Harassment at Workplace (Prevention) Act
Every organization must have an Internal Complaints Committee. Covers all women employees, visitors, and domestic workers. Mandatory awareness programs required.
Act 2013
IPC Section 354 – Assault on Women
Assault or criminal force against a woman with intent to outrage her modesty. Punishable with imprisonment of 1-5 years and fine. Cognizable and non-bailable offense.
IPC
IPC Section 376 – Rape
Minimum punishment of 10 years rigorous imprisonment, extendable to life imprisonment. Fast-track courts established for speedy trials. Medical examination within 24 hours mandatory.
IPC
Nirbhaya Fund Schemes
Government fund for initiatives improving safety of women. Covers safe city projects, one-stop centers, women helpline, CCTV in cities, forensic labs, and fast-track courts.
2013
IT Act – Cyberstalking & Online Harassment
Section 66A and 67: Publishing obscene material and cyberstalking are criminal offenses. Report to cybercrime portal: cybercrime.gov.in. Helpline: 1930
IT Act 2000
Zero FIR Rule
You can file an FIR at any police station regardless of jurisdiction. The station must register and transfer it. Police cannot refuse to register an FIR — this is your right.
